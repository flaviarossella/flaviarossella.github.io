he story of the castle begins in the heart of the Middle Ages, when the lands were a mosaic of fiefs and mysterious woods. The castle was raised as a stronghold and noble residence, amid the clash of weapons, wandering knights, and ladies wrapped in velvet cloaks. In the early twentieth century, the destiny of Grazzano Visconti turned into a fairy tale: Duke Giuseppe Visconti di Modrone, a man of artistic vision and a nostalgic heart, decided to bring the ruins back to life and rebuilt the village and the castle as a hymn to ancient medieval splendor. It was not just a restoration but the creation of a dream: cobbled streets that seem to lead to other times, carved portals like pages from a chivalric poem, towers that touch the sky like fingers in love. It is said that within those rooms still dwells the spirit of gentle Aloisa, a kind lady whose fate was broken by a lost love. Many say they have seen her walking on moonlit nights, wrapped in a light cloak, as if still searching for a heart capable of listening to her story.
